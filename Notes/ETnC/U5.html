Unit 1: Electrical Safety

Safety for Domestic Electrical Appliances

Electrical appliances are vital in households but can pose risks such as electric shocks, fires, or damage if improperly handled. Following safety practices is essential to prevent accidents.

Do’s:

  • Read the User Manual: Always follow the manufacturer’s operation, maintenance, and safety precautions instructions.
  • Use Proper Wiring: Ensure appliances are connected to properly grounded outlets and correctly wired circuits.
  • Check for Damage: Regularly inspect cords, plugs, and outlets for signs of wear, damage, or fraying.
  • Turn Off After Use: Switch off and unplug appliances when not in use.
  • Install Circuit Protection: Use Residual Current Devices (RCDs) or circuit breakers.

Don’ts:

  • Don’t Press Unknown Switches: Avoid pressing switches unless sure of their function.
  • Don’t Tamper with Electrical Equipment: Never modify wiring unless qualified.
  • Don’t Work on Live Circuits: Only authorized personnel should handle live circuits.

Industrial Electrical Safety

During Operation:

  • Wear PPE: Use insulated gloves, face shields, and safety shoes.
  • Follow Lockout/Tagout (LOTO): Shut down and de-energize equipment before maintenance.
  • Use Insulated Tools: Handle live equipment with insulated tools.

During Maintenance:

  • De-Energize Equipment: Isolate electrical systems before maintenance.
  • Test for Voltage: Use voltage testers before working.

Rescue Procedures for Electric Shock

  • Isolate the Victim: Remove the person safely from the electrical source.
  • Cut Power: Switch off the circuit breaker.
  • Start CPR: If the person is not breathing, begin CPR immediately.
  • Call for Emergency Help: Seek professional medical assistance.

Fire Safety

Types of Fire:

Class Type of Fire Extinguisher Types
Class A Ordinary combustibles (wood, paper, cloth) Water, Foam, Powder
Class B Flammable liquids (petrol, diesel, grease) Foam, Powder, CO2
Class C Flammable gases (propane, butane) Powder

Fire-Fighting Equipment:

  • Fire Extinguishers: Choose based on fire type.
  • Fire Hoses: Used for large fires.
  • Fire Blankets: Smother small fires.
